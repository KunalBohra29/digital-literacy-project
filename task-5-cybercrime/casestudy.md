# Case Study – Identity Theft

## What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft is a cybercrime in which an attacker illegally obtains and uses another person's personal information — such as their name, Aadhaar number, PAN card details, bank account credentials, or date of birth — without their knowledge or consent, typically for financial gain or to commit fraud.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Data Collection:** The attacker gathers personal details through phishing emails, fake websites, data breaches, or by purchasing stolen data on the dark web.
2. **Verification Bypass:** Using the victim's details (name, date of birth, mobile number), the attacker answers security questions or bypasses OTP verification by SIM swapping — convincing the telecom provider to transfer the victim's number to a new SIM.
3. **Account Takeover:** With access to the victim's mobile number and personal details, the attacker resets passwords on banking, UPI, or e-commerce accounts.
4. **Financial Exploitation:** The attacker transfers funds, applies for loans, makes purchases, or opens new credit accounts in the victim's name.
5. **Cover-Up:** The attacker changes contact details in compromised accounts, delaying the victim's discovery.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

College students, elderly individuals, and people with limited digital awareness are most commonly targeted. Students are targeted because they frequently sign up on new platforms, often reuse passwords, and may not monitor their financial accounts closely.

## Consequences

- Financial loss that can take months or years to recover.
- Damaged credit score affecting future loan eligibility.
- Legal complications if the stolen identity is used to commit crimes.
- Significant emotional distress and loss of trust in digital platforms.
