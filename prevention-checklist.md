# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

## Identity Theft & Cybercrime Prevention for College Students

---

### 🔐 General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps. This adds a second layer of security even if your password is stolen.

3. **Never share personal documents** (Aadhaar, PAN, college ID) over WhatsApp, email, or social media unless absolutely necessary and verified.

4. **Regularly check your accounts** — review your bank statements, email login history, and app permissions at least once a month for suspicious activity.

5. **Do not click on unknown links** in SMS, WhatsApp, or emails — even if they appear to come from your bank, college, or a government body. Always visit official websites directly.

6. **Keep your software and apps updated** — security patches in updates protect against known vulnerabilities that attackers exploit.

---

### 💸 UPI & Financial Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone** — no bank, government body, or UPI app will ever ask for your PIN. Anyone asking for it is a fraudster.

8. **Be cautious of "collect money" requests on UPI** — scammers send fake payment requests disguised as receiving money. Entering your PIN on a collect request sends money OUT of your account, not into it.

---

### 🆔 Identity Protection

9. **Monitor your Aadhaar usage** by checking your authentication history at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in to detect any unauthorized use of your identity.

10. **Do not photograph or post your ID cards, marksheets, or boarding passes** on social media — these contain personal details that can be exploited.

---

### 🚨 If You Are a Victim — Report Immediately

- 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** (available 24x7)
- Report as early as possible — faster reporting increases the chance of recovering lost funds.
