# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Do** keep your profile information professional — use a clear name and appropriate profile photo.
2. **Do** think before you post — ask yourself if you would be comfortable with a future employer seeing this.
3. **Do** use privacy settings to control who can see your personal posts and information.
4. **Do** give credit when sharing others' content — respect intellectual property.
5. **Do** use social media to network professionally — follow industry leaders, professors, and companies in your field.
6. **Do** report abusive, threatening, or inappropriate content using the platform's reporting tools.
7. **Do** log out of shared or public devices after using social media accounts.

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't** share your phone number, home address, or financial details publicly on any platform.
2. **Don't** post negative comments about your college, professors, or classmates — it reflects poorly on you professionally.
3. **Don't** accept friend/follow requests from people you do not know.
4. **Don't** share OTPs, passwords, or banking details over social media messages — even if the sender appears to be someone you know.
5. **Don't** post or forward unverified news and rumours — always fact-check before sharing.
6. **Don't** use your social media passwords for other accounts — use unique passwords for every platform.
7. **Don't** overshare your daily routine or location in real time — this can be a safety risk.
